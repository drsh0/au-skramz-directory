<p><b>Last Edited:</b> 28/12/2025 </p>

<p>Formed in 2015 as Late Nite TV in Lennox Heads, before eventually changing their name to Project Jurassic, in which they would find themselves opening for bands such as 
Wharves & Eliza and the Delusionals, and Coming 2nd place in a Battle of the Bands competition in Tweed Heads, all before releasing a Song. In Late November 2017, 
Project Jurassic would officially announce a name change to Garage Sale, and release their debut song <a href="https://dogclubrecords.bandcamp.com/track/fubar">FUBAR</a> 
at the start of December 2017. The band at this point would have the Lineup of Dan Sullivan (Vocals, Guitar), Evan Mcgarrity (Bass) & Miller Brand (Drums), with the artwork 
for this single having been made by Benji Luke. Live sets with the original 3 piece lineup are basically gone to time, however <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Ok1NSlDeukk">
some footage</a> does still exist.</p>
<br>
<p>At some point between then & 2019, Benji would become a full time member of the band, as Garage Sale would release their first EP, "You Don't Have to Grow Up". Whilst this EP 
is mostly scrapped from the internet, <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VF53HRfwImo">some songs</a> still exist online. The <a href"https://gyro.lnk.to/YouDontHavetoGrowUp?fbclid=IwY2xjawORvntleHRuA2FlbQIxMABicmlkETFTOVdoeXFaTUhsUDlUQlNhc3J0YwZhcHBfaWQQMjIyMDM5MTc4ODIwMDg5MgABHnOf_qdqjW57g19eJXzocFeojWagpu31okzrtOExIhd5AJCia3KkD138t9CT_aem_MCYU15idwAu52P9ULqQEqA">
linktree</a> for the EP still exists as well, and whilst the links no longer lead to the songs, the previews still exist. This year would see the band start to tour more around the 
East Coast of Australia, Having played Festival of the Sun, Hot Tropics & Performing 2 different tours for the People I Don't Know Single & and the Home Single, which spanned across 
Wollongong -> Brisbane.  7news Coast even gave them a little Interview at Festival of the Sun, and TripleJ would <a href="https://www.abc.net.au/triplej/news/get-outta-town:-5-artists-from-outside-the-big-smoke-you-gotta/11503368?fbclid=IwY2xjawOckHhleHRuA2FlbQIxMQBzcnRjBmFwcF9pZBAyMjIwMzkxNzg4MjAwODkyAAEevBIJg8GcMcJnVKoquiwnqcmhsCwfeCoWk5VWFy8bYS-L53HUGF5Fet97ZXo_aem_D3UrCdLXknnWahBS9m4TDw">
feature them</a> in a 5 bands you should hear article.</p>
<br>
<p>Very early 2020 would see them play in Melbourne for the first time, and over the COVID years, the band would find themselves moving to Melbourne, with Catey Ellis officially 
joining the band at the end of the year. This lineup would be the start of the new sound for Garage Sale, with the debut single from the album "Frank" coming out in early 2021. 
Further singles in 'Bus' & 'Shoes On' would be dropped before the group's debut album 'Shimmer' was released in July 2022. A year later, the band also released the standalone single
'Blank Again', which would be the final song with Marci Aiello on Drums. Ellis Fleming would go on to record drums on the Stars Fall 2 EP, with David Quested (Local The Neighbour)
officially joining the band sometime after the recording. As of December 2025, this is the current lineup for Garage Sale.</p>
